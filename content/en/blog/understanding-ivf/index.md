In Vitro Fertilisation (IVF) is one of the most well-known and effective fertility treatments available today. Whether you're just starting to explore your options or have been on this journey for a while, understanding the IVF process can help you feel more confident and prepared.

## What is IVF?

IVF is a type of assisted reproductive technology (ART) where eggs are collected from the ovaries and fertilised with sperm in a laboratory. The resulting embryo is then transferred to the uterus, where it can implant and develop into a pregnancy.

## Who Can Benefit from IVF?

IVF may be recommended for couples or individuals experiencing:

- Blocked or damaged fallopian tubes
- Ovulation disorders
- Endometriosis
- Male factor infertility (low sperm count or motility)
- Unexplained infertility
- Age-related fertility decline

## The IVF Process

1. **Ovarian stimulation** — Medications are used to stimulate the ovaries to produce multiple eggs
2. **Egg retrieval** — Eggs are collected through a minor procedure under sedation
3. **Fertilisation** — Eggs are combined with sperm in the lab
4. **Embryo development** — Embryos are monitored for 3–5 days
5. **Embryo transfer** — One or more healthy embryos are placed into the uterus
6. **Pregnancy test** — A blood test is done approximately two weeks after transfer
